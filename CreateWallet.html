Create Your Digital Wallet

IMPORTANT! PLEASE FOLLOW INSTRUCTIONS CAREFULLY!

Step 1

ChekOut tokens are built on the Ethereum Blockchain. You can learn more about Ethereum HERE. Since it's built on the Ethereum Blockchain, you need a wallet that supports these type of tokens. The easiest, and safest way to do so is by using MyEtherWallet at the button below. Be sure to bookmark the link and ONLY use the bookmark to prevent from going to a fradulent site. Do this now and then come back for Step 2.

MyEtherWallet

Step 2

(These Instructions Are Paraphrased From the MyEtherWallet Website. For a More Detailed Explanation Click HERE. You Can Also Watch the Video Tutorial HERE.)

A. On MyEtherWallet homepage enter a strong but easy to remember password. Do not forget it.

B. Click the "Create New Wallet" button.

C. Download your Keystore / UTC file & save this file to a USB drive. This is the encrypted version of your private key. You need the password to access it. 

D. Read the warning. If you understand it, click the "I understand. Continue" button.

E. Print your paper wallet backup and/or carefully hand-write the private key on a piece of paper.

F. Copy & paste your address into a text document somewhere.
Search your address on https://etherscan.io/ and paste in your address. Bookmark this page. This is how you will view your balance at any time.

G. Unlock your new wallet from the Keystore / UTC file on your USB drive or via the private key that you printed out. Ensure it unlocks the wallet you are sent to and matches the bookmark you have.

Step 3

BACKUP EVERYTHING!

A. Place your Keystore / UTC file, as well as the text document that contains your wallet address on the same USB drive (if you haven't already done so). If you feel you may forget your password, then copy and paste that into the text document as well. However, it is best to simply have your password memorized and only stored in your head. 

B. I HIGHLY recommend backing up the contents of this USB drive onto TWO ADDITIONAL USB drives.

C. Now that you have three USB drives with identical information, store them in three completely separate places. And no, storing one in your bedroom, one in the bathroom and one in your living room is NOT three separate places. If an emergency such as a fire were to occur, you would be COMPLETELY locked out of your fundsWe cannot tell you where to store them, but please make sure they are in completely different locations.
